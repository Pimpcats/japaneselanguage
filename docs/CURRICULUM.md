# はなそう (Hanasou) — Full Curriculum

A speaking-first Japanese course: English prompt → say it in Japanese out loud → reveal, hear the model answer, self-grade. Generated from `lessons.js` — the single source of truth for all content.

## How the curriculum is built

- **One grammar point per lesson.** Sentences only recombine that lesson's vocab and grammar; function words (です, ます, particles) are taught by the grammar note, never listed as vocab.
- **Level 0 teaches the alphabet through words**: each micro-lesson introduces one gojūon row (5 kana) and only uses letters taught so far. Romaji fades automatically once a word's letters are known.
- **Difficulty ramp**: 5 words + 5 sentences per lesson at the start, growing one step per stage (6/6, 7/7, 8/8 …) toward 10/10 as levels advance.
- **Vocabulary rule**: early levels only use words a visitor would say in their first month — everyday and immediately useful.
- Sentences are kana-first; kanji only appears with furigana brackets (かんじ[reading]).

# Level 1 — Foundations

## Tier: First steps
*First words & survival phrases*

### Theme: First Contact (5 lessons)

#### Hello & Introductions `intro`

**Grammar point:** X は Y です  ·  か

> は marks the topic (what you're talking about) — written は (ha) but always SAID wa. です means is/am. Add か at the very end to turn a statement into a question.

**Vocabulary (6):**

| Japanese | Romaji | English | Part of speech |
|---|---|---|---|
| わたし | watashi | I / me | noun |
| がくせい | gakusei | student | noun |
| アメリカじん | amerika-jin | American | noun |
| ともだち | tomodachi | friend | noun |
| なまえ | namae | name  (お = polite) | noun |
| なん | nan | what | noun |

**Sentences (6):**

- Nice to meet you. — **はじめまして。** *(hajimemashite.)*
- I'm Tom. — **わたしは トムです。** *(watashi wa Tomu desu.)* — hint: は marks the topic
- I'm American. — **わたしは アメリカじんです。** *(watashi wa amerika-jin desu.)*
- I'm a student. — **わたしは がくせいです。** *(watashi wa gakusei desu.)*
- What's your name? — **おなまえは なんですか？** *(onamae wa nan desu ka?)* — hint: か makes it a question
- My friend is a student. — **ともだちは がくせいです。** *(tomodachi wa gakusei desu.)*

#### Greetings & Courtesies `greetings`

**Grammar point:** あいさつ — set phrases

> These greetings never change form — learn each as one chunk. ございます makes おはよう and ありがとう more polite.

**Vocabulary (9):**

| Japanese | Romaji | English | Part of speech |
|---|---|---|---|

| こんばんは | konbanwa | good evening | expression |
| ありがとう | arigatou | thank you | expression |
| ございます | gozaimasu | (makes a phrase polite) | auxiliary |
| すみません | sumimasen | excuse me / sorry | expression |
| さようなら | sayounara | goodbye | expression |
| はい | hai | yes | expression |
| いいえ | iie | no | expression |

**Sentences (8):**

- Good morning. — **おはよう ございます。** *(ohayou gozaimasu.)* — hint: ございます = polite
- Hello. — **こんにちは。** *(konnichiwa.)*
- Good evening. — **こんばんは。** *(konbanwa.)*
- Thank you very much. — **ありがとう ございます。** *(arigatou gozaimasu.)*
- Excuse me. / I'm sorry. — **すみません。** *(sumimasen.)*
- Yes, that's right. — **はい、そうです。** *(hai, sou desu.)* — hint: そう = so / that way
- No, that's wrong. — **いいえ、ちがいます。** *(iie, chigaimasu.)*
- Goodbye. — **さようなら。** *(sayounara.)*

#### This, That & “Whose” `this-that`

**Grammar point:** これ・それ・あれ  ·  の

> これ = this (by me), それ = that (by you), あれ = that (over there), どれ = which. の links two nouns: わたしの ほん = my book.

**Vocabulary (7):**

| Japanese | Romaji | English | Part of speech |
|---|---|---|---|
| これ | kore | this | noun |
| それ | sore | that (by you) | noun |
| あれ | are | that (over there) | noun |
| どれ | dore | which one | noun |
| ほん | hon | book | noun |
| かばん | kaban | bag | noun |
| わたしの | watashi no | my / mine | noun |

**Sentences (7):**

- What is this? — **これは なんですか？** *(kore wa nan desu ka?)*
- This is a book. — **これは ほんです。** *(kore wa hon desu.)*
- That (by you) is a bag. — **それは かばんです。** *(sore wa kaban desu.)*

- Is that (by you) a book? — **それは ほんですか？** *(sore wa hon desu ka?)*

**Grammar point:** 〜さい  ·  なんさいですか

> Age is a number plus さい: きゅうさい = nine years old. Ten squeezes to じゅっさい. Ask with なんさいですか. Numbers past ten stay regular — にじゅうごさい is 25. (Twenty years old has a special word — it comes later.)

**Vocabulary (6):**

|---|---|---|---|
| なんさい | nan-sai | how old | noun |
| さい | sai | years old (counter) | noun |
| こども | kodomo | child | noun |
| おとな | otona | adult | noun |
| わかい | wakai | young | adjective |
| とし | toshi | age / years | noun |

**Sentences (6):**

- How old are you? — **なんさいですか？** *(nan-sai desu ka?)*

- My friend is nine years old. — **ともだちは きゅうさいです。** *(tomodachi wa kyuu-sai desu.)* — hint: 〜さい = years old
- How old is your friend? — **ともだちは なんさいですか？** *(tomodachi wa nan-sai desu ka?)*
- I'm twenty-five years old. — **にじゅうごさいです。** *(nijuu-go-sai desu.)* — hint: past ten it stays regular
- The child is five years old. — **こどもは ごさいです。** *(kodomo wa go-sai desu.)*

### Theme: The て-form (3 lessons)

#### The て-form `te-form`

**Grammar point:** verb → 〜て

> The て-form is the connector verb. Build it from the dictionary form: る-verbs drop る (たべる→たべて); う/つ/る→って, む/ぶ/ぬ→んで, く→いて, ぐ→いで, す→して. する→して, くる→きて, いく→いって (irregular). On its own it's a quick casual request: みて！ = “look!”

#### Although / That Said (〜ものの・〜とはいえ) `monono`

**Grammar point:** 〜ものの  ·  〜とはいえ

> 〜ものの = “although / even though” (formal): やくそくしたものの、いけなかった = “although I promised, I couldn't go.” 〜とはいえ = “that said / even though”: ふゆとはいえ、あたたかい = “even though it's winter, it's warm.”

**Vocabulary (7):**

| Japanese | Romaji | English | Part of speech |
|---|---|---|---|
| ものの | mono no | although | conjunction |
| とはいえ | to wa ie | that said / even though | conjunction |
| やくそく | yakusoku | promise  (約束) | noun |
| ふゆ | fuyu | winter  (冬) | noun |
| さんせい | sansei | agreement / approval  (賛成) | noun |
| たより | tayori | reliance  (頼り) | noun |
| あたたかい | atatakai | warm | adjective |

**Sentences (5):**

- Although I promised, I couldn't go. — **やくそくしたものの、いけなかった。** *(yakusoku shita mono no, ikenakatta.)*
- Even though it's winter, it's warm today. — **ふゆとはいえ、きょうは あたたかい。** *(fuyu to wa ie, kyou wa atatakai.)*
- It's convenient, that said, I'm not used to it yet. — **べんりとはいえ、まだ なれない。** *(benri to wa ie, mada narenai.)*
- Although I understand, I can't agree. — **わかるものの、さんせいできない。** *(wakaru mono no, sansei dekinai.)*
- Even though he's young, he's reliable. — **わかいとはいえ、たよりに なる。** *(wakai to wa ie, tayori ni naru.)*

#### As Far As / While (〜かぎり・〜つつ) `kagiri`

**Grammar point:** 〜かぎり  ·  〜つつ  ·  〜にほかならない

> 〜かぎり = “as far as / as long as”: しるかぎり = “as far as I know.” 〜つつ = formal “while”: しりつつ = “while knowing.” 〜にほかならない = “is nothing other than”: どりょくのけっかにほかならない = “it's nothing but the result of effort.”

**Vocabulary (6):**

| Japanese | Romaji | English | Part of speech |
|---|---|---|---|
| かぎり | kagiri | as far as / as long as  (限り) | conjunction |
| つつ | tsutsu | while  (formal) | conjunction |
| にほかならない | ni hokanaranai | is nothing other than | auxiliary |
| どりょく | doryoku | effort  (努力) | noun |
| けっか | kekka | result  (結果) | noun |
| せいこう | seikou | success  (成功) | noun |

**Sentences (5):**

- As far as I know, it's true. — **しるかぎり、ほんとうだ。** *(shiru kagiri, hontou da.)*
- As long as I'm alive, I'll keep trying. — **いきているかぎり、がんばる。** *(ikite iru kagiri, ganbaru.)*
- Knowing it's bad, I still can't stop. — **わるいと しりつつ、やめられない。** *(warui to shiritsutsu, yamerarenai.)*
- Success is nothing but the result of effort. — **せいこうは どりょくの けっかに ほかならない。** *(seikou wa doryoku no kekka ni hokanaranai.)*
- As far as I can see, there's no problem. — **みるかぎり、もんだいない。** *(miru kagiri, mondai nai.)*

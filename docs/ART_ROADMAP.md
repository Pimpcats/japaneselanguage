# はなそう — Map Art Roadmap (Donkey-Kong-style journey)

Every theme in the curriculum becomes one painted **location panel**. Lesson
nodes will hop across your painting like a board-game trail, so each place IS
the study area for what it teaches. Produce them in any order — the moment the
first panel lands in `assets/map/`, the positioning system gets wired and the
rest slot in as they arrive.

## Global spec (applies to every panel)

| | |
|---|---|
| **Orientation / size** | Portrait, **1600×2400 px** (delivered), shown at ~800×1200 CSS px |
| **Format** | JPG (scenes without transparency) ~quality 80, target ≤ 500 KB — I can compress on arrival |
| **Style anchors** | Match `assets/sign.png`, `assets/street_soft.jpg`, the chibi cat art: soft watercolor fills + warm ink outlines, hand-drawn, kawaii but not childish |
| **Palette** | The app's cream/pink/gold world: cream sky `#fff7ea`, pink accents `#ef6fa7`, gold `#f2be45`, ink `#4a3328`, pastel greens/blues for nature |
| **The path zone (critical)** | A clearly visible winding road/path from the **bottom edge to the top edge**, roughly centered (may weave left/right within the middle ~60%). Keep it **low-detail and low-contrast** — 3–6 ink-outlined node dots and their labels must sit ON it and stay readable |
| **No text in the art** | Names/labels are rendered by the app |
| **Light** | Consistent warm daylight unless the brief says otherwise |
| **Naming** | Exactly as listed below, into `assets/map/` |

**Bonus panels (optional, later):** `home-street.jpg` — the shop street hub
behind the level-select screen; `japan-bg.jpg` — a painted replacement for the
SVG Japan overview map.

## Level 1 · Foundations — the home town

**L1-1 · `first-contact.jpg` — もち子さん's Shop Front**
*Teaches:* greetings, introducing yourself, this/that, numbers (4 lessons)
*Paint:* the entrance to the shop street — a small torii gate or noren curtain
gateway, もち子's shop with its striped awning right at the start of the path.
Warm, welcoming, morning light. Props: a welcome mat, a shop cat, a hand-drawn
"open" sign feel (no text).

**L1-2 · `getting-things.jpg` — The Market Lane**
*Teaches:* shopping, money & prices, counters, where-is, the café (5 lessons)
*Paint:* stalls and little storefronts on both sides — fruit stand, a café with
outdoor seats, hanging price tags (blank/scribbles, no real text), coin purse,
baskets. The path winds between the stalls.

**L1-3 · `doing-things.jpg` — The Town Square & Station Front**
*Teaches:* verbs, を objects, negatives, coming & going (4 lessons)
*Paint:* a small open square in front of a tiny train station — people mid-verb:
someone eating, someone reading, someone waving goodbye, a bus pulling in.
Movement everywhere.

**L1-4 · `daily-life.jpg` — The Residential Lane**
*Teaches:* daily routine, telling time, frequency, weekend activities (4 lessons)
*Paint:* a quiet homes-and-gardens lane; a prominent **clock tower** or big
station clock as the landmark, laundry on balconies, a bicycle, morning bustle.

**L1-5 · `the-past.jpg` — The Old Shrine**
*Teaches:* past tense, past negative, was/were, sequences (4 lessons)
*Paint:* stone steps up to a small weathered shrine — moss, old ema plaques,
autumn leaves; a place with a memory. Slightly golden late-afternoon light.

**L1-6 · `wants-plans.jpg` — The Festival Street**
*Teaches:* want-to, let's, likes & dislikes, plans (4 lessons)
*Paint:* matsuri evening — paper lanterns strung overhead, food stalls
(taiyaki, ringo-ame), a happi-coat crowd feel. "I want to eat THAT." Dusk blues
with warm lantern glow (keep the path zone readable).

**L1-7 · `describing.jpg` — The Lookout Hill**
*Teaches:* adjectives, adjective+noun, な-adjectives, negation (4 lessons)
*Paint:* a grassy hilltop viewpoint above the town — the whole colorful world
below to describe: big/small, old/new, pretty. A bench, a telescope, sakura
tree framing the view.

## Level 2 · Connecting & doing

**L2-1 · `te-form.jpg` — The Bridge**
*Teaches:* the て-form itself (3 lessons)
*Paint:* a red arched bridge over a river — the connector, literally. Koi in
the water, stepping stones leading onto it. The path crosses the bridge.

**L2-2 · `linking-ideas.jpg` — The Riverside Path**
*Teaches:* linking actions & requests with て (5 lessons)
*Paint:* a willow-lined riverside walk where the water (and the path) flows in
one continuous line — small waterwheel, ducks, stones linked like beads.

## Level 3 · Getting around

**L3-1 · `finding-your-way.jpg` — The Train Station**
*Teaches:* directions, transport, how-far (4 lessons)
*Paint:* a charming country train station — platform, a friendly green train,
hanging route map (scribble lines, no text), signposts pointing everywhere.

**L3-2 · `tickets-trouble.jpg` — The Ticket Gate**
*Teaches:* buying tickets, handling hiccups (3 lessons)
*Paint:* inside the station — ticket machines with glowing buttons, turnstiles,

## Level 4 · Real conversations

**L4-1 · `talking-casually.jpg` — The Yatai Alley at Night**
*Teaches:* casual/plain speech (4 lessons)
*Paint:* narrow alley of steaming food carts, stools, red lanterns, friends
elbow-to-elbow laughing. Nighttime warmth — the casual register as a place.

**L4-2 · `reacting-relating.jpg` — The Onsen Town Street**
*Teaches:* reacting, relating experiences (3 lessons)
*Paint:* a steamy hot-spring town lane — wooden inns, foot-bath bench with two
chatting bathers ("そうですか！"), steam curling, towels and geta.

## Level 5 · Nuance & plans

**L5-1 · `if-necessity.jpg` — The Mountain Fork**
*Teaches:* conditionals, must/have-to (3 lessons)
*Paint:* a forest trail that visibly **forks** around a big signpost stone —
if you go left…, you must go right… Misty pines, two diverging paths that
rejoin toward the top (the node path follows one side).

**L5-2 · `plans-impressions.jpg` — The Ryokan Inn**
*Teaches:* intentions, how things seem (4 lessons)
*Paint:* a traditional inn at dusk — engawa veranda, futon airing, a packed
travel bag, itinerary scroll (scribbles); planning the journey ahead.

## Level 6 · Expert

**L6-1 · `things-happen.jpg` — The Bamboo Forest**
*Teaches:* passive & causative — things done TO you (3 lessons)
*Paint:* tall bamboo closing over the path, dappled light, a fox statue half
seen, wind-blown leaves — a place where things happen to you.

**L6-2 · `explaining-nuance.jpg` — The Tea House**
*Teaches:* explaining yourself, fine nuance (4 lessons)
*Paint:* a refined little tea house interior/garden — tatami, a tokonoma
alcove, whisk and bowl mid-ceremony, precise and calm.

## Level 7 · Master

**L7-1 · `keigo.jpg` — The Castle Gate**
*Teaches:* keigo & formal politeness (3 lessons)
*Paint:* a white castle keep above a grand gate, pine trees, a bowing figure —
the domain of formal language. Stately, airy.

**L7-2 · `native-connectives.jpg` — The Summit Shrine at Dawn**
*Teaches:* native-level connectives (4 lessons)
*Paint:* the final climb — a mountaintop torii at sunrise above a sea of
clouds, the whole journey's world tiny below. The last stop on the road.

## Delivery & wiring plan

1. You drop any finished panel into `assets/map/` (or send it to me) — start
   with **L1-1 or L1-2** since that's where daily practice happens.
2. I wire the node-positioning system on the first panel: per-location node
   coordinates (like the Japan-map pins), path styling on top of the art,
   preload + service-worker caching.
3. Each further panel is then pure data: filename + 3–6 node coordinates.
4. Themes without art yet keep the current trail styling — mixed states are
   fine; nothing blocks on the full set.

19 panels total (72 path stops) · 7 for Level 1, 2 each for Levels 2–7 (+2 optional bonus).

How it works ▾

Each card shows an English line — say it out loud in Japanese, then tap 🎌 reveal & hear to check the model answer and hear it spoken.

The answer is colour-coded by word role. Below it, every word is a chip — tap one to hear that word, or tap ↗ to look it up on Jisho.

Grade yourself nope / kinda / got it. Anything but “got it” comes back for review on its own schedule; got it locks the card in.

Sentence structure ▾

One rule: the verb goes last. Everything before it adds detail, and each detail wears a particle tag that says its role.

who it's aboutは wa
the thing acted onを o
destination / timeに ni
where / by whatで de
starting pointから kara
together withと to
… then the verb 🎯

タロウは としょかんに いきました — “Tarō went to the library.”

Word colours ▾

In the answer and the word chips, each word is tinted by its job:

noun verb adjective adverb particle auxiliary conjunction expression

Particles (は・を・に…) and the copula です share the same grey, since they're grammatical glue rather than meaning.
